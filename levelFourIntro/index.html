Stage 3 of Ocean Pollution Reduction: Plastic Bans

One of the biggest ways to prevent plastic from getting into the ocean in the first place is a national total ban on single use plastics. Places in America that have been able to pass legislation to ban single use plastics have seen drastic changes in their local ecosystems and environments in even short amounts of time, pairing this with cleanup efforts and a more efficient and non profit driven national recycling program and we have a way to effectively prevent most plastic from getting into the ocean in the first place. A 2022 poll showed that 81% of American voters are in favor of legislation banning or reducing single use plastic use, so why haven’t we done this? One of the biggest things standing in the way of this progress is the immense power held by the oil and plastic lobby, who spend more than $85 million yearly to reduce restrictions on plastic production and recycling. This key part of the solution to the oceanic plastic crisis is to reduce or completely eliminate the power held by lobbyists in government, especially those in the oil and plastics industries who will spend whatever is necessary to ensure their bottom lines aren’t harmed by planet saving measures.

Viability: Difficult, but Doable

As mentioned, petrochemical lobbyists will pose significant opposition to single-use plastic bans. However, with enough public support, it would still be possible to pass. The creation of better alternative materials through the previous stage's government incentive programs will help, as people will be familiar with alternatives to plastic and will be more likely to feel that plastics are unecessary.
