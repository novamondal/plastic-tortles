Stage 2 of Ocean Pollution Reduction: Plastic Alternatives

Putting into place government incentive programs to fuel research into biodegradable plastics or plastic alternatives is an important step of our plan to get rid of ocean plastic, and hopefully plastic in general. Currently, some bioplastics are not even completely biodegradable and have the same issues of pollution and creation of microplastics as regular plastics, and also cost much more to produce and some kinds require special facilities to make them. Government incentives to reduce the costs of this process and make all bioplastics biodegradable. Also looking into complete alternatives such as paper bags etc. is an important part of eliminating plastic in our oceans.


To encourage the development of more sustainable alternatives to plastics, the US government should create incentive programs and scientific research grants for companies developing disposable plastic alternatives that are truly biodegradable. These alternative materials currently include biodegradable resin straws and bamboo utensils, for example, but new materials would also be developed. This will help incentivize innovation and should bring down the end cost to consumers, since the R&D costs will be subsidized by the government. Lower end cost of plastic alternatives should encourage higher adoption, leading to less usage of disposable single-use plastics. It also opens the door to other legislative options in the future.

Viability: Relatively Viable

The plan should be popular with the public, as it helps create new industries and the subsidies would help create jobs in the renewable materials industry. Funding for the incentive programs could be a roadblock, but this funding could easily come out reducing current subsidies of other, less beneficial industries such as industrial corn farming or oil subsidies.
