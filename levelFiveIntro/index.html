Stage 4 of Ocean Pollution Reduction: Plastic Eating Bacteria

Scientists have developed bacteria that can digest plastics and break them down into harmless substances, or into raw materials that can easily be recycled into new plastics out of plastics that are not traditionally recyclable. However, there are some problems with these bacteria in their current form.

Viability: Not Currently Viable, but Promising

In its current state, biodepolymerizing bacteria is not a completely viable solution to the oceanic plastic crisis. Most of our solutions attempt to strike at the source of the pollution, through bettering recycling programs or having government incentives to recycle more effectively, but few are able to combat the garbage already present in the ocean. French company Carbios has introduced a modified genome that can break down 90% of a plastic bottle in room temperature conditions, but this takes 6 hours and shows the inefficiency of the process as of now. Many are also concerned about the idea of releasing genetically modified bacteria into the environment. One of the biggest issues with the degrading enzymes is that they may not degrade ALL of the plastic, leaving microplastics behind. However on a positive note, this is a very new field of study and is rapidly advancing, so we may see these problems remedied in the future.


Although major development is still needed for plastic-eating bacteria to be truly viable, it is a promising development for the distant future of reversing oceaning pollution.
